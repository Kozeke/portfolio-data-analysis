Project 1: Visualizing the COVID-19 Data for the Asia

Stack:Python, d3.js

Visualizing the impact of Covid-19 in Asian Countries. Used charts: pie charts, buble chart, line chart, scatter plot, choropleth map, stacked bar chart

Usefull Insights:

  • Insights reveal that Georgia and Armenia experienced a higher death rate compared to other countries, with lower vaccination rates, GDP, and hospital beds.
  • Age and COVID-19 Correlation: A scatter plot analyzes the correlation between the share of the population aged 65 and older and the share of the population with confirmed COVID-19 cases. Generally, there is a positive correlation, suggesting that countries with older populations tend to have fewer confirmed cases. Some outliers, like Brunei and Bahrain, deviate from this trend.
  • Impact of Vaccination on COVID-19 Spread: A choropleth map compares the number of deaths and vaccine rollout in different countries. High-GDP countries with more vaccines show lower COVID-19 cases.
    • Lockdown Measures and Stringency Index:
  • A choropleth map uses a stringency index to depict the impact of government-imposed lockdown measures in Asian countries. The stringency index is based on metrics such as school closures, workplace closures, and travel restrictions.
  • Case Fatality Rate (CFR) Analysis: CFR is explored over different periods, noting fluctuations during the initial, third, and fifth waves. In the initial and fifth waves, GDP does not significantly affect CFR, but there is some correlation in other waves. Human development index and the availability of hospital beds impact CFR in later periods.

Project 5: World Cup Analysis (1930-2014)

Tableau

This project delves into the rich history of the FIFA World Cup from 1930 to 2014, utilizing a comprehensive dataset. The primary objective is to extract valuable insights and trends, showcasing them through interactive data visualizations created using Tableau. The dataset encompasses a wide range of information, including match statistics, player performances, and tournament outcomes.

  • 1. Top Scorers Visualization (Bar Chart)

    • The project identifies and highlights the top 15 goal scorers across all World Cup tournaments from 1930 to 2014.
    • A visually engaging bar chart presents the goal-scoring prowess of these football legends.

  • 2. Host Country and Winner Relationship (Bar Chart):

    • Investigating the correlation between the host country and the eventual winner.
    • A bar chart vividly illustrates the historical connection between hosting privileges and clinching the championship.

  • 3. Correlation Between Matches Played and Goals Scored (Scatter Plot):

    • Examining the correlation between the number of matches played and the total goals scored in each tournament.
    • A scatter plot provides an insightful visualization of how goal-scoring trends correspond to the intensity of matches played.

  • 4. Goals Scored Home and Away (Map Visualization):

    • Mapping the countries with the highest number of goals scored when playing on home turf and away.
    • Interactive maps offer a geographical representation of goal-scoring prowess in both home and away matches.

  • 5. Best Teams Home and Away in Extra Time (Bubble Chart):

    • Identifying the best teams in extra time, both at home and away, by considering wins through penalties or goals.
    • A dynamic bubble chart visualizes the performance of teams, with the bubble size representing the number of wins in extra time.
